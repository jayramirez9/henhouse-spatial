## B004 — Structural grid (E01-chassis-contract)
- **Date**: 2026-06-09
- **Epic**: E01-chassis-contract
- **What**: Defined the structural grid from Premier SIPS load charts (ICC-ES ESR-4524, IBC/IRC compliant). **Key finding: the SIP shell is grossly over-strong (~17× axial margin) — the real engineering problem is local mounting, not gross structure.** Foam core holds no fasteners and 7/16" OSB skin has low withdrawal, so heavy cartridge loads (LED, camera grid) must land on embedded lumber. Specified: panel/seam layout (large panels, fewer seams) with spline type as a design lever (Type S connection-only/no-bridge default; **Type L lumber splines placed deliberately at LED-rail and camera-grid lines as mounting backers**; Type I for roof). Three load paths — gravity (roof→walls 3,373 plf cap vs ~190 plf demand→floor→deck→frame), Mode B crane-lift (needs integral steel sub-frame for ISO castings), and road-dynamic (2-3g, fatigue — outside chart scope, prototype required). Mounting grid as cartridge interface: walls ≥10 psf hung via embedded ledgers, ceiling ≥250 lb hard points, floor ≥100 psf assembly (steel deck is the real structural floor).
- **Files**: contracts/chassis-contract.md (structural grid section, replaces placeholder), configs/content-node.md (LED mounting → hard points, not bare skin), tasks.md, builds.md
- **Eval**: Each surface (wall/ceiling/floor) has a mounting method + capacity grounded in manufacturer load data. Spline-type selection mapped to thermal-vs-structural tradeoff. Load paths cover both deployment modes + the non-standard transport case. Content Node LED mounting updated to require chassis hard points.
- **Flags**: **Mode B (crane-off) adds steel sub-frame weight** — conflicts with the B003 weight finding; question whether Mode B earns its weight for unit #1 or gets deferred like the battery bank. **Road-dynamic/fatigue loading is outside the SIP manufacturer's static chart scope** — seams and mounts need a transport factor and must be prototyped/instrumented before fleet production.
- **Status**: Complete

## B005 — HVAC + ERV system spec (E01-chassis-contract)
- **Date**: 2026-06-16
- **Epic**: E01-chassis-contract
- **What**: Sized ventilation + supplemental HVAC for the Content Node worst case. **Headline finding: cooling governs (not heating), and the cooling load is bounded by the electrical service, not the envelope.** Nearly all studio electrical power becomes in-room heat, so the load is capped by the 50A/9,600 W-continuous service — and the AC draws ~3,000 W from that same budget. HVAC, the heat it rejects, and the power service are circularly coupled; the 50A service is the *governor* on studio thermal output. Worst-case cooling ≈ **29,200 BTU/hr (~2.4 tons)** = power-capped equipment (~6,450 W = 22,000 BTU/hr, after HVAC+ERV reserve ~3,150 W of the 9,600 W continuous service) + 10 occupants (4,500) + post-ERV ventilation (700) + envelope/sol-air (2,000). Sized to **3 tons (36,000 BTU/hr)** for margin/pull-down. Selected: **variable-capacity (inverter) ducted mini-split heat pump** (inverter mandatory for empty-box→full-studio turndown; single-stage would short-cycle and not dehumidify) + **150 cfm enthalpy-wheel ERV** (sized for 10-occupant CO2 control, exceeds ASHRAE 62.1 minimum). Heating is trivial (~2,000 BTU/hr loss in the passive envelope). Defined single-zone layout, mechanical-zone equipment location, duct routing + register method (low face velocity for noise), NC-25 acoustic target tied to the Taycan-quiet benchmark, condenser exterior-rejection requirement, and Heavy-Air Lounge airflow accommodation. HVAC+ERV weight firmed at **~280 lbs**.
- **Files**: contracts/chassis-contract.md (HVAC section replaces placeholder; locked decisions +ERV/HVAC rows; weight budget ERV/HVAC firmed + systems subtotal note; status banner), configs/content-node.md (thermal load validated + power cross-constraint), tasks.md, builds.md
- **Eval**: Cooling load itemized with a defensible basis for each line; tonnage justified against the power budget, not guessed; equipment selected with power, weight, and a stated reason (inverter, enthalpy wheel); weight tracked against the chassis systems budget; zone/duct/register method defined with interface coords deferred to B007; acoustic + condenser + Lounge constraints surfaced.
- **Flags**: **⚠️ Binding cross-constraint → B006/B011: the 50A service, not HVAC tonnage, caps the studio's heat/brightness/volume.** After HVAC+ERV reserve their draw, only ~6,450 W continuous remains for the entire cartridge. B006 must reserve HVAC/ERV load before cartridge circuits; B011 must itemize Content Node to fit. Naive full-white LED (~14 kW) is physically unsupplied by 50A. If a future battery/V2L lifts the ceiling, studio load AND required HVAC rise together. **Condenser packaging** (exterior rejection of ~45,000 BTU/hr) and **duct depth penalty** (low-velocity ducts compete with LED/acoustic depth in the 6'8" box) carry to B007/B010.

## D002 — Exterior Width decision (E01-chassis-contract)
- **Date**: 2026-06-16
- **Type**: Decision record (revises B002/B003 envelope outputs — not a new build)
- **Decision**: **Adopt 8'6" (102" / 2591mm) exterior width** for the GA-metro fleet, up from the original 8'0" (96"). Walls stay 6½" GPS SIP (R-28). New interior: **~7'3" (2210mm) as-built / 7'2" (2184mm) design target**, up from 6'9"/6'8" — a clean +6".
- **Regulatory basis**: Georgia's statewide legal max vehicle/load width is **102" (8'6")** under O.C.G.A. § 32-6-23, on all public roads — **no oversize permit** for local delivery in Atlanta, Fayetteville, Peachtree City, Newnan. Over 102" needs a permit (§ 32-6-28). One operational catch: **102"-wide vehicles may only travel on 12-foot-lane roads** — arterials/highways qualify; narrow residential/rural *final approaches* are the risk (per-venue route check). Build to 102" with margin (trim/skins/awnings must not exceed it).
- **Why this beats the wall-thinning alternative**: the +6" interior comes entirely from the wider exterior, so **R-28 walls are retained** — no thermal sacrifice. The earlier "thin walls to 6", recover R in the roof" path is moot. The binding spatial constraint loosens for every cartridge; Content Node's LED + acoustic depth budget now holds the ≥5'6" audience target with margin even at 4" panels.
- **Trade accepted**: gives up the universal "any state, any road" margin that 8'0"/96" guaranteed. 102" is permit-free in GA and most states, but a few cap local roads at 96", so out-of-state delivery is no longer guaranteed permit-free. Justified by the Atlanta-south-metro local delivery model.
- **Files**: contracts/chassis-contract.md (locked decisions, dimensions table, new Regulatory: Transport Width subsection, width budget, wall-assembly table + decision, dimensional summary, weight-budget +90 lb note, HVAC zone/duct refs), configs/content-node.md (interior width, floor area, density, panel-depth math, width budget), CLAUDE.md (dimensional constants), masterplan.md, README.md, implementation-plan.md, tasks.md, builds.md
- **Consequences to honor**: (1) 102" envelope adds ~90 lbs (wider roof/floor/end walls) — fold into the envelope subtotal at B007; still within the ~5,750 lb cartridge allowance. (2) Trailer frame **and deck** must be 102"-capable (not a stock 96" deck under a 102" box) — tasks open item 5. (3) **Open: validate 12'-lane final approach per booked venue as a go/no-go booking gate**; re-check local-road rules if the fleet leaves GA — tasks open item 4. (4) The GA 102"/§ 32-6-23 basis is **[ASSUMED] pending GDOT confirmation** — web-sourced, not yet verified with the permit office. (5) Height budget unchanged (width-only decision) — though the fab diagram's stale interior-height figure was corrected to the B003 value (~10'1"–10'7") in passing.
- **Status**: Decided
